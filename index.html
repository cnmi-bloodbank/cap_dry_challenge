DRY CHALLENGE (JE1)

Dry challenges are PT/EQA specimens that are provided as an opportunity for laboratories to perform an analysis when it is not possible to provide a physical specimen.

CASE STUDY

A 45-year-old male with interstitial lung disease was admitted for double lung transplant. His pre-operative hemoglobin was 10.2 g/dL (reference range: 13.7 – 17.5 g/dL). The patient had no history of transfusions or red cell alloantibodies. Blood bank testing performed with automated column agglutination technology on the morning of surgery showed Group A, Rh-positive blood type and a negative antibody screen. The cadaveric organ donor was Group O, Rh-positive. The patient received two units of packed red blood cells (RBCs) in the perioperative period. No other blood products were administered.

On post-operative day 10, the patient appeared jaundiced. Laboratory testing revealed indirect hyperbilirubinemia, an elevated lactate dehydrogenase, and an abrupt decrease in hemoglobin from the day prior (from 9.0 g/dL to 6.9 g/dL).
